Q1. What is digital forensics?
Digital forensics is the process of collecting, preserving, analyzing, and presenting digital evidence in a legally admissible manner.

Q2. Why are hashes critical in forensics?
Hashes verify integrity and prove evidence was not altered.

Q3. Why analyze disk images instead of live systems?
To prevent evidence modification and ensure repeatable analysis.

Q4. What is chain of custody?
Documentation that tracks evidence handling from acquisition to reporting.

Q5. How does Autopsy help investigations?
It enables file system analysis, deleted file recovery, keyword search, timeline reconstruction, and reporting.

Q6. How are deleted files recovered?
From unallocated space if data blocks remain intact.

Q7. What is timeline analysis?
Chronological reconstruction of file system events using metadata.

Q8. Why are plaintext passwords dangerous?
They indicate poor security practices and credential exposure risk.

Q9. What makes a report court-ready?
Verified hashes, documented steps, preserved integrity, and clear findings.

Q10. Who uses these skills in real life?
SOC analysts, DFIR teams, law enforcement, and compliance auditors.
